Medical Oncology: An Overview

*Medical oncology* is a branch of medicine that focuses on the diagnosis, treatment, and management of cancer. Oncologists use a variety of therapies, including chemotherapy, hormone therapy, targeted therapy, and immunotherapy, to treat cancer. The primary goal is to eradicate cancer cells, reduce tumor size, and prevent the spread of the disease, while also providing supportive care to improve the patient's quality of life.

Description: Uses drugs to destroy cancer cells. These drugs can be administered orally or intravenously.

Purpose: To kill rapidly dividing cancer cells, shrink tumors, and prevent metastasis.

Common Drugs: Paclitaxel, Doxorubicin, Cisplatin, Cyclophosphamide.

Description: Involves the use of hormones or hormone-blocking drugs to treat certain cancers, particularly breast and prostate cancers.

Purpose: To slow or stop the growth of hormone-sensitive tumors.

Common Drugs: Tamoxifen, Letrozole, Anastrozole, Leuprolide.

Take a look at this

Description: Utilizes drugs or other substances that specifically target cancer cells' unique genes, proteins, or tissue environment.

Purpose: To attack cancer cells while minimizing damage to normal cells.

Common Drugs: Trastuzumab, Imatinib, Bevacizumab, Erlotinib.

Take a look at this

Description: Empowers the body's immune system to recognize and combat cancer cells.

Purpose: To enhance the natural defenses of the immune system against cancer.

Common Drugs: Pembrolizumab, Nivolumab, Ipilimumab, Atezolizumab.

Take a look at this

Description: Replaces damaged or destroyed bone marrow with healthy stem cells.

Purpose: To restore the body's ability to produce blood cells after intensive cancer treatment.

Common Drugs: Autologous (using patient's own cells) and allogeneic (using donor cells).

Take a look at this

Description: Involves the use of living organisms, substances derived from living organisms, or synthetic versions to treat cancer.

Purpose: To boost or restore the body's natural defense mechanisms.

Common Drugs: Monoclonal antibodies, cytokines, cancer vaccines.
